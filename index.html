About me

Studies of European Archaeology, Philosophy, Physical and Cultural Anthropology and Computer Science at the University of Freiburg (2008-2012, 2013-2019), University of Texas at Austin (2012-2013) and PUC University Rio de Janeiro (2005-2007). I am specially interested in the historico-genetic theory of culture.

There will soon be a new Podcast project with the Uni-Radio uniFM 88,4 coming. So, stay tuned.

I am presently a Post-Doc Researcher at the Department of History of the Universidade Federal Fluminense as well as a Requirements Engineer for the ILIAS Learning Platform at the E-Learning Department of the Computer Center at the University of Freiburg in a project called bwILIAS. Previously, I offered as IT-Admin technical support in IT-Infrastructure to research and teaching at the Institute of Sociology and the Computer Center (Rechenzentrum) for several scientific research groups and institutions of the same University. I also taught Portuguese I, II & III at (ZfS and SLI) at the Univ of Freibrug, where I instructed on lusophone language and culture. Other lectures and classes or talks on archaeology can be found under publications

An enthusiast of the use of technology in the field of archaeology, I enjoy learning with courses in Computer Sciences (B.Sc.) at the University of Freibrug - whenever I find the time.

My hobbies include managing this website, playing guitar (Jazz, Bossa Nova and MPB) and learning Japanese.
